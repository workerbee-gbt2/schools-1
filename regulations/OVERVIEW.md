# Regulations Overview

This directory contains information about California Interscholastic Federation (CIF) regulations and medical eligibility requirements for school sports physicals.

- [Medical Eligibility Form Details](medical_eligibility.md): History, purpose, and specific PPE forms used for sports participation.

## CIF Regulations: Sports Physicals
Based on the CIF Constitution and Bylaws (500 Series: General Rules) and the CIF Sports Medicine Handbook, the following are key regulations for student-athletes:

1. **Annual Requirement**: Student-athletes must undergo a physical examination annually (defined as within the last 365 days) [1](#ref-1), [3](#ref-3).
2. **Medical Certification**: The examination must be conducted by a medical practitioner certifying that the student is physically fit for interscholastic athletics [1](#ref-1), [3](#ref-3).
3. **Mandatory Timing**: The physical must be completed **before** any tryout, practice, or competition [1](#ref-1), [3](#ref-3).
4. **Approved Documentation**: Results must be recorded on a form approved by the local school board and include a health history [1](#ref-1), [3](#ref-3).
5. **Recommended Form**: The CIF Sports Medicine Advisory Committee (SMAC) strongly recommends the [Pre-Participation Physical Examination (PPE) form](medical_eligibility.md) endorsed by major medical societies [2](#ref-2).
6. **Exemptions**: Exceptions may apply for parent/guardian refusal to consent under specific Education Code provisions [1](#ref-1).
