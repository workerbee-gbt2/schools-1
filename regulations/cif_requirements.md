# CIF Medical Eligibility Requirements

## Overview
This document covers the California Interscholastic Federation (CIF) requirements for student-athlete physicals, governed by the California Education Code.

## Key Requirements
Student-athletes in California are required to undergo a [Preparticipation Physical Evaluation (PPE)](medical_eligibility.md) to determine their medical eligibility for sports participation [1](#ref-1).

## California Education Code Sections

### Physical Examinations
When a school district or county superintendent requires a physical examination for participation in an interscholastic athletic program, the examination may be performed by a physician and surgeon or a physician assistant [2](#ref-2).
*   **Code Section**: EDC Section 49458

### Concussion and Head Injuries
Athletes suspected of sustaining a concussion must be removed from activity and cannot return until evaluated and cleared in writing by a licensed healthcare provider [3](#ref-3).
*   **Code Section**: EDC Section 49475

### Sudden Cardiac Arrest
Under the Eric Paredes Sudden Cardiac Arrest Prevention Act, any pupil who passes out or exhibits symptoms of sudden cardiac arrest during or after athletic activity must be cleared by a physician, nurse practitioner, or physician assistant before returning to play [4](#ref-4).
*   **Code Section**: EDC Section 33479.5

## CIF Bylaw 503.G: Physical Examinations
As a condition of membership, CIF schools must require students to receive an annual physical examination [5](#ref-5).

*   **Timing**: The physical must be completed before a student may try out, practice, or participate in interscholastic athletic competition [5](#ref-5).
*   **Validity**: An annual physical examination is defined as one completed within the last 365 days [5](#ref-5).
*   **Certification**: A medical practitioner must certify that the student is physically fit to participate in athletics [5](#ref-5).
*   **Form Requirements**: The report must be on a school board-approved form and must include a health history [5](#ref-5).
*   **Exemptions**: Students may be excused if they comply with Education Code provisions regarding parent/guardian/caregiver Refusal to Consent [5](#ref-5).
*   **Recommendations**: The CIF Sports Medicine Advisory Committee strongly recommends using the Pre-Participation Physical Examination Form endorsed by major medical societies [5](#ref-5).
